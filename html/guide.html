Thinking about a Kei truck?

Pick the truck that matches how you’ll actually use it

Kei trucks look tiny and benign, but they are real workhorses. Before you import one or buy one locally, it helps to know how you plan to use it and what kind of setup fits your life.

This page gives a quick overview of some popular Kei truck models and the types of builds they are usually used for.

Example Kei Truck Setups

These are just example builds to get ideas going. A lot of Kei trucks can be used in more than one way.

Real Kei Truck Models and Common Uses
Model Drive Best For Pros Things to Consider
Honda Acty 4WD (farm style) 4WD Small farm work, acreage, hauling tools and feed Simple and reliable, flat bed is easy to load, good fuel mileage for daily chores Most Actys are older now, so check for rust and plan on slower highway speeds
Suzuki Carry DA63T (lifted build) 4WD Trails, camping trips, mild off road exploring Popular platform for lift kits and bigger tires, tons of parts and aftermarket support Lifted trucks ride stiffer on pavement and aggressive tires are louder and wear faster
Daihatsu Hijet (street mini truck) RWD / 4WD Street builds, cars and coffee, cruising around town Great for lowered “mini truck” style builds, tons of character, easy to park anywhere Lowered trucks have less ground clearance and can scrape on speed bumps and driveways
Subaru Sambar (topper + racks) 4WD Light contractor work, handyman setup, mobile tool hauler Works well with toppers and roof racks, enclosed bed keeps tools out of the weather Added topper and racks add weight, so expect slightly less fuel mileage and performance

Of course there's many more makes and models. These are just very common ones. No matter which model you like, double check your state or local laws for on road use, and try to get a pre purchase inspection before you buy.
