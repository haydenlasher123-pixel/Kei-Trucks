Thinking about a Kei Truck?

Kei trucks look tiny and cute, but they are real workhorses. Before you import one or buy one locally, it helps to know how you plan to use it and what kind of setup fits your life.

Below is a quick guide to common Kei truck setups, what they are good at, and what to watch out for.

Example Kei Truck Setups

Kei Truck Builds and Their Strengths
Setup Drive Main Use Pros Things to Consider
Stock farm truck 4WD Hauling tools, feed, and lumber Simple, reliable, good fuel mileage Lower ground clearance, not great for deep ruts
Lifted off-road build 4WD Trails, camping, hunting trips More clearance, fun on forest roads Harsher ride on pavement, tires wear faster
Street style mini-truck RWD Shows, cars and coffee, cruising Super unique, easy to park in cities Low ride height can scrape on speed bumps
Work topper with racks 4WD Contractor, handyman, mobile shop Lockable storage and ladder racks Extra weight, slightly lower fuel mileage

No matter which setup you choose, always check your state or local laws for on-road use, and get a pre-purchase inspection if you can.
